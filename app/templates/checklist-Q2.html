Question #2

Is the website’s domain name trustworthy?

Next Question
  • Hints
  • Examples

Look at the website’s URL (address), eg The University of Sydney is www.sydney.edu.au. The domain name follows the organisation’s name. For example, in the University of Sydney’s URL, the organisation’s name is ‘sydney’ and the domain name is ‘edu’. ‘au’ means the organisation is in Australia.

A domain name tells you that there may be a conflict of interest, or potential bias, between the owner of the website and its content.

Common domain names are .gov, .org, .edu, .com and .net:

  • .gov is for websites of hospitals, government organisations and non-profit organisations. These are normally trustworthy websites that help the public. They normally have little or no conflict of interest or bias.
  • .org is also for websites of hospitals, government organisations and non-profit organisations (eg www.trapeze.org.au). These are normally trustworthy websites that are for the public. They normally have little or no conflict of interest or bias.
  • .edu is for websites of universities, schools and other educational institutions (eg www.sydney.edu.au). These are normally trustworthy sites that are for the public. They normally have little or no conflict of interest or bias.
  • .com is for websites of private, commercial companies that are usually for-profit. Some of these sites may have a conflict of interest or bias, but not necessarily. For example, WebMD (www.webmd.com) and Reachout (www.reachout.com) are popular and generally trusted websites that have little or no conflict of interest or bias.
  • .net is for companies involved in networking technologies (eg www.iinet.net.au). They are also a general purpose domain name for other websites, for example Raising Children, which is supported by the Australian Government (www. raisingchildren.net.au). Some of these websites may have a conflict of interest or bias, but not necessarily. For example, the website of the Australian Government-funded ABC (Australian Broadcasting Corporation) (www.abc.net.au) provides health information and has no conflict of interest or bias.

A good .gov example is Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. (CDC) The CDC is a part of the United States of America's Department of Health and Human Services.
